The most Terrifying Exotic Animals on Planet Earth

By Cooper Robbins

Do you know about exotic animals? No, not your pet cat, dog, or your basic goldfish. I'm talking about real exotic animals that you don't see every day, or ever for that matter. Well, they are out there and some of which are too dangerous to encounter. Even for humans, some of these exotic creatures can cause severe, or fatal, injuries if provoked in the wild. If you haven't guessed already, here are some of the most terrifying exotic animals to ever walk, or swim, on planet Earth.

The Nile Crocodile

Found throughout Africa is the most aggresive type of crocodile on planet Earth; the Nile Crocodile. First of all, this beast is afraid of nothing and is the second-largest crocodile in the world, with the largest ever recorded at a whopping 21 feet, 2 inches long, and weighed around 2,400 pounds! Secondly, these exotic creatures have between 64 and 68 teeth and pack a powerful bite. You do not want to come in contact with these creatures because the result can be fatal. Nile Crocodiles usually drown their prey underwater first, until they precede to start tearing flesh off. Lastly, the Nile Crocodile is an Apex Predator, meaning that they eat all sorts of fish, birds, mammals, and other reptiles. Humans can, and will be apart of their meal prep too, if you are not careful. You should be okay to look at them from a distance, only because they often prey on animals that drink water at the edge of rivers, marshs, swamps, or lakes. Animals, or any acceptable food source for that matter, that drink from the edge usually end up getting snatched by Nile Crocodiles, starting the meal prep stage mentioned earlier. The chances of surviving the powerful bite(s) of the Nile Crocodiles, escaping the drowning phase, and getting away without any serious or life-threatening injuries is very, very low. So, it's best to keep a distance from these exotic creatures, the farther the better, because your life could end up depending on it.

The Blue-Ringed Octopus

This exotic ocean dweller is native to the Pacific Ocean and is a lot more terrifying than it looks. With venom that is 1000 times more powerful than any other octopus, the Blue-Ringed Octopus is considered one of the most deadliest animals in the ocean. This exotic creature has enough venom to kill more than 25 humans within minutes. What's even crazier is that there is no known cure for this little guy's venom, making it very dangerous if you are bitten by them. When they feel threatened, the Blue-Ringed Octopus activates the blue rings all around its body as a warning signal to visitors or other ocean dwellers. When you are unfortunate enough to be bitten by one, you won't feel anything at first, but after a couple of minutes you will start feeling the side effects of the venom. First, the venom will block your nerve signals all throughout the body causing your muscles to numb. Your body will likely go into muscle paralysis. Then, you will experience nausea, vision loss, and loss of basic motor skills because of the numbness of your muscles. Finally, you will likely go into respiratory arrest due to your important breathing muscles going numb. If you immediately know that you have been bitten by the Blue-Ringed Octopus, it is best you call 911 as soon as possible so that they can arrive to assist you before the side effects kick in. However, there hasn't been any recorded deaths from this creature since the 1960's, so if you ever see this exotic creature, its best to look from a distance and never get too close, especially if you start to see the blue rings.

The Wolverine

This exotic animal inhabits mainly timbered areas in the colder parts of North America and Eurasia. This animal my be small, but it is very terrifying considering the different animals and mammals it preys on. This is the Wolverine, and as mentioned above, the Wolverine feasts on all sorts of animals including those more than twice it's own size. Here are some of the animals Wolverines feast on:

  • Deer
  • Elk
  • Sheep
  • Moose
  • Wolves
  • Small Bears
  • Other Livestock

The Wolverine also has long, sharp claws and is very strong and ferocious. Because of this, the Wolverine is fearless and able to fight, or fight off, almost any predator. The powerful claws are able to tear flesh right off the victims body, while then using it's strong teeth for further dissembly. Although the Wolverine is terrifying when provoked,that is generally all that will occur if you come in contact with one; you will only provoke it. Wolverines, fortunately for you, are generally not known to actively hunt or attack humans. But, they will if you somehow threaten or corner it. With this exotic animal, it is also best to keep a distance from it because if provoked, the Wolverine can do some serious damage.
